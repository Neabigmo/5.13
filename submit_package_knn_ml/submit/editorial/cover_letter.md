We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Leave-One-Out Stability Does Not Certify Replacement Robustness in k-Nearest Neighbors," for consideration in *Machine Learning*.

The paper studies a finite-sample stability question for deterministic k-nearest-neighbor classification. Leave-one-out evaluation is often interpreted as evidence that a classifier is insensitive to individual training points. We show that this interpretation conflates two different perturbation semantics: LOO deletes an occurrence and evaluates at that same occurrence, while replacement robustness permits arbitrary replacement and arbitrary query evaluation.

Our contributions are: (i) a precise perturbation calculus separating delete-one, insert-one, replace-one, and LOO evaluation conventions; (ii) an exact signed-margin crossing criterion for deterministic k-NN; (iii) finite metric constructions showing zero LOO indicators but a replace-one prediction flip for every odd k and for even k under deterministic tie-breaking; and (iv) reproducible experiments and diagnostics illustrating the mechanism beyond the analytic witnesses.

We believe the manuscript is appropriate for *Machine Learning* because it clarifies the interpretation of a classical validation/stability diagnostic for a canonical learning rule, while connecting finite-sample theory with reproducible empirical analysis.
