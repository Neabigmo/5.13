\title[LOO stability and replacement robustness in k-NN]{Leave-One-Out Stability Does Not Certify Replacement Robustness in k-Nearest Neighbors}

\author*[1]{\fnm{First} \sur{Author}}\email{email@example.com}
\affil*[1]{\orgdiv{Department}, \orgname{Institution}, \orgaddress{\country{Country}}}

\abstract{Leave-one-out evaluation is often interpreted as evidence that a classifier is insensitive to individual training points. We show that this interpretation fails for deterministic $k$-nearest-neighbor classification, because LOO deletion is tied to evaluation at the deleted point, whereas replacement robustness concerns arbitrary replacement and arbitrary query evaluation. For every odd $k \ge 1$, and for even $k$ under deterministic tie-breaking, we construct finite metric samples for which all LOO indicators vanish but a single replace-one perturbation flips the prediction at a designated query. The separation is governed by a signed top-$k$ vote margin: LOO perturbations can leave the margin on the safe side of the decision threshold, while a targeted replacement crosses it. Experiments and a margin-based diagnostic show that this phenomenon is observable beyond hand-crafted witnesses.}

\keywords{k-nearest neighbors, algorithmic stability, leave-one-out, robustness, finite metric spaces, perturbation analysis}
