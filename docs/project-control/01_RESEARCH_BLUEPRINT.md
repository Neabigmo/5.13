# Research Blueprint

## Core Questions

1. What general relationships hold among delete-one, add-one, replace-one, LOO, pointwise, expected, and uniform stability?
2. Are uniform delete-one and replace-one stability equivalent up to constants under bounded loss?
3. Exactly when does deterministic k-NN change prediction after deleting or replacing a sample?
4. Can finite graph shortest-path metrics separate 1-NN LOO stability from replace-one stability?
5. What are the smallest witnesses and how do they depend on labels and tie-breaking?
6. How do the separations extend to arbitrary k?
7. How do worst-case finite examples coexist with classical k-NN consistency and prediction guarantees?

## Target Theorems

- Uniform no-separation theorem.
- Exact k-NN perturbation criterion.
- Minimal finite graph separation for 1-NN.
- k-NN gadget lifting.
- Compatibility with classical consistency.
- Optional consequence for conformal prediction stability certificates.

## Milestones

1. Freeze definitions.
2. Implement auditable finite metric and k-NN code.
3. Reproduce small graph enumeration.
4. Produce witness JSON, figures, and tables.
5. Convert computational witnesses into proof-reviewed lemmas.
6. Complete literature table and novelty assessment.
7. Compile a paper draft.
