# Figure Captions

- Figure 1. Perturbation semantics. LOO couples deletion to evaluation at the deleted occurrence, whereas replace-one decouples both the edited occurrence and the evaluation query.

- Figure 2. Margin mechanism. The constructive separations move the signed top-k vote margin from -1 to +1 for odd k and from 0 to +2 for even k under deterministic tie-breaking.

- Figure 3. Synthetic graph-metric benchmark. Separation frequency and vulnerable-query rate are computed from the full reproducible benchmark aggregates.

- Figure 4. Tabular benchmark. Separation frequency and vulnerable-query rate are summarized across the UCI-style binary tabular datasets with 95% confidence intervals.

- Figure 5. Stability hierarchy. Uniform replace-one can be bounded through delete-one plus insert-one, but pointwise LOO does not control pointwise replace-one.
