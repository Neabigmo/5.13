\section{Uniform Stability Calculus}
\label{sec:uniform-calculus}

At the level of perturbation operations, replace-one is the composite move
obtained by deleting an occurrence and then inserting a new labeled point.
That observation is almost tautological at the sample level, but it is worth
writing down explicitly because it yields the correct direction of implication
for worst-case stability control.

    \centering

\begin{proposition}[Uniform replace-one through delete-plus-add]
\label{prop:replace-delete-add}
Fix a deterministic learning rule $A$ that maps ordered samples to predictors
and a bounded loss $\loss \in [0,1]$. Suppose that for every sample $S$, every
index $i$, every evaluation pair $(x,y)$, and every admissible appended labeled
point $z$,
\[
\left|\loss(A(S),(x,y))-\loss(A(S^{-i}),(x,y))\right| \le \beta_{\del}
\]
and
\[
\left|\loss(A(T),(x,y))-\loss(A(T\oplus z),(x,y))\right| \le \beta_{\add}
\]
uniformly over all intermediate samples $T$. Then every replace-one
perturbation satisfies
\[
\left|\loss(A(S),(x,y))-\loss(A(S^{i\leftarrow z}),(x,y))\right|
\le \beta_{\del}+\beta_{\add}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For fixed $S,i,z,(x,y)$, let $T=S^{-i}$. By construction,
$S^{i\leftarrow z}=T\oplus z$ up to the repository's fixed convention that the
new occurrence is inserted after the deletion. Therefore
\begin{align*}
\left|\loss(A(S),(x,y))-\loss(A(S^{i\leftarrow z}),(x,y))\right|
&\le
\left|\loss(A(S),(x,y))-\loss(A(T),(x,y))\right| \\
&\quad +
\left|\loss(A(T),(x,y))-\loss(A(T\oplus z),(x,y))\right|
\end{align*}
by the triangle inequality, and the claimed bound follows from the two uniform
assumptions.
\end{proof}

\Cref{prop:replace-delete-add} is deliberately abstract. It is not specific to
$k$-NN, and it does not yet tell us whether the bound is informative for a
particular local learner. Its role is to freeze the algebra of perturbation
types before we zoom in on the exact combinatorics of deterministic nearest
neighbors.

The proposition also clarifies what it does \emph{not} say. A pointwise LOO
indicator cannot simply be dropped into the role of delete-one control above,
because LOO changes the evaluation point along with the sample. Similarly, a
fresh-test or expected-loss stability statement from the classical literature
does not automatically transfer to the worst-case fixed-query indicators used
in the present paper. The whole point of the calculus is to prevent these
category mistakes.
